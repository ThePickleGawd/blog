Book Notes

Austin Kleon Series

Notes on some of Austin Kleon's books. This post is part of a series where I review what I learned from each book I read.

Dylan Lu

Jun 1, 2023 • 1 min read

These are some notes I found in an old notebook. I am typing these up on 6/1/23 (last day of Junior year!)

Steal Like an Artist - Austin Kleon

Finished 10/1/22

Great ideas are "stolen" from others

  • Don't copy, but instead, add interesting twists
  • Nothing is "original"
  • Take ideas from people you like/inspire you

Start now, and limit yourself

  • Too many possibilities can be bad. Pick useful things
  • Don't make excuses, no "holidays". Stay consistent and the work WILL get done

Filter out bad ideas and people

  • There are so many yideas, but you need to filter out the bad ones
  • Surround yourself with smart people and hang out with them. If you're the smartest in a room, change rooms.
  • Your salary is close to the average of your 5 closest friends. Choose wisely

Keep Going - Austin Kleon

Finished 10/2/22

When you feel unmotivated, make a gift for someone else

  • You'll feel more excited about making something for someone other than yourself

Forget "being a coder", do coding now

  • Stop watching Youtube videos on how to be the best coder. Start coding, and start it now

Keep a consistent schedule. Life is like groundhogs day

  • Worry about today
  • Plan ahead and make todo lists

Keep your tools nearby, but your work can be scattered and all over the place

  • Make sure you can find the tools you need. Keep them organized
  • Work can be messy. Don't slow down

Go for a walk, it clears your head

  • Naps are also good
  • Drink caffeine and then nap. When it kicks in, get back to work

Be the change you want to see

  • Write the book you want to read

Isolate from the world sometimes

  • "Bliss Stations"
